    Project Brief

    Understand

    The Market

    The Buyer

    • Primarily for new, small-scale property buyers who are looking to invest for additional income or financial security.
    • Provide first-time buyers access to reliable, uncomplicated information about their potential property investments.
    • Aid buyers in conducting property searches and making decisions about where to invest.
    • Be able to search for properties anywhere; on mobile, tablet, or desktop.

    Persona

    Meet Riley

    Riley is a busy consultant who is frequently on the go. She is a pro at multitasking and relies heavily on technology to help her manage her tasks. Riley is looking to invest in properties beyond the city for increased financial security for her family.

    As she is new to real estate, she wants an app that helps her find the properties within her search scope. She needs to find comprehensive information for fast decision-making so as not to waste her time.

    Ideate

    How can Riley use this app to find investment properties? How can Riley use this app with a trusted real estate agent throughout the home-buying process? Answering these questions helped me figure out which features to include in the first version of this app.

    Then, I spoke to friends and family about their experience with selling and buying homes. I was able to identify a few key features that were important to buyers and agents across the board. These interviews helped me determine how people would like to navigate through Olive.

    Based on my interviews and my exploration of a few popular apps, these are a few initial user flows for Riley.

    Design Decisions

    Design Evolution

    Deciding on a Palette

    While I think there is a lot of appeal for both versions of Heritage, I have decided to continue with the second mood board because it more closely aligns with the project brief.

    “I want to provide my family with financial security. I’ve been considering buying property for a while, and am looking for a tool that can help me find what I’m looking for, quickly!” -Riley (Primary Persona)

    White is a great way to communicate a calm, simple, and professional atmosphere to users. I decided on shades of blue as my primary color because it evokes feelings of calm, serenity, and trust. Warm accent tones create an atmosphere of stability and strength. The balance of cool and warm tones set to a white background will support Heritage’s professional and positive attitude towards helping everyone find the perfect property.

    Learnings

    • You don't need to reinvent the wheel. Familiar designs can improve the user experience.
    • When in doubt, keep it simple.
    • Feeling stuck? Take a break and come back with fresh eyes.
    • Save everything. You never know when an old idea might circle back around!

    Moving Forward

    Keep Designing

    As I continue with my UX Immersion course, I want to create more screens. I’m interested in interviewing more buyers and real estate agents to flush out details.

    I would begin with more user interviews to understand how people feel about each stage of the home buying process. I also want to know how agents manage multiple properties and clients. Finally, learn more about what features would enhance efficient communication between buyers, sellers, and agents.

    There are many competitors to Heritage, but I think through more user interviews and usability testing, Heritage can find a niche market to cover.

    Reflection

    It's Okay to Take a Break

    You know when you look at a word for too long and you're not sure it's even a word? Well, that's how I felt multiple times throughout this project. I'm learning to permit myself to take a break without feeling like I'm falling behind.

    Ask Questions

    Unlike my first UX project, I didn't go through as many stages of research. At times I felt a bit lost and unsure of my design direction. In these times, I reminded myself to check in with others and ask for help thinking through the process.

    Inspiration is Everywhere

    A vast majority of people have some experience buying or selling a home or know someone who has. You never know when someone you see daily could offer insights into a design decision that feels difficult to solve.
