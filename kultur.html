Kultur

Intro to the World of UX

The Intro to UX Design course introduced me to important UX terms, tools, and techniques. With so many new terms and concepts to learn, it seemed fitting that the course project would be a vocabulary learning project.

While researching how other experience learning new terms, I was able to pick up a few tricks while I tackled UX course vocabulary.

  • Role: Student UX/UI Designer
  • Time: October 2020 - November 2021
  • Tools:
  • Special Thanks: Todd Chapin (mentor), Jonathan Boehr (tutor)

Design Criteria

It can be hard to keep track of new jargon. People need a way to categorize, reference, and learn and master new vocabulary and concepts.

Design a mobile application focused on learning new vocabulary on the go. Design should include the following:

  • A ​splash screen​, including a logo and tagline
  • An ​onboarding process​ or experience
  • A way to ​sign up and log in​ that allows users to input and save their information
  • An ​admin area​ where users can access their information
  • A ​menu​ that lets users navigate the application
  • A way to​ allow users to upload​ new vocabulary words with personal definitions
  • A way to review vocabulary

Our Persona

I’d like you to meet Sloan, our user persona. She’s hardworking, adventurous, creative, and extremely witty. She loves her job at a tech startup, especially the collaborative environment. She is a self-proclaimed foodie who is always down to try the newest restaurant in town. When she isn’t working or out with friends, she can be found knee-deep in planning her next adventure.

She finds that she learns best when there is variety and even better when she feels incentivized. She often is most motivated right before a trip or when a friend is also learning the same language.

User Needs

Sloan needs a new way to learn that doesn’t feel generic. A place that is uniquely her own and will allow her to customize what and how she learns.

The majority of the apps on the market focus on making users proficient, not taking into consideration other motivations to learn a new language such as simply wanting to learn food vocabulary, slang, or enough to sing along to their favorite Swedish song.

User Flow & Task Analysis

To understand how Sloan might navigate through Kultur, I created a user flow for three possible tasks.

  1. Adding own vocabulary
  2. Collaborative learning
  3. Setting friendly reminders

Conducting a task analysis helped me narrow down which screen to design for first as I moved into bringing my ideas to life through sketch.

Wireframing & Prototyping

We now have a persona, a problem, and the primary tasks necessary to reach a potential solution. Next, it was time for wireframing and building a low-fidelity prototype for testing.

It took me some time to get started sketching. The task felt a little daunting and, I wasn't sure where or how to start. I remembered reading that screens should feel familiar, so I recreated standard login screens to help me get drawing.

Usability Testing

Next, we ran usability tests with our target users. The test consisted of four tasks:

  • Sign-up/onboarding
  • Adding a new word
  • Playing a game with a friend
  • Setting a new reminder

Having no previous experience with usability testing, I wasn't sure what to expect. I was most looking forward to getting feedback on my idea. In my head, it made sense. Now it was time to see if my design was as straightforward as I thought it was.

Design Glitches & Revisions

Next Steps

Emphasis the benefits of learning in a customizable and collaborative spaces.

Encourage users to use the CHAT function to practice their written and spoken skills while language learning.

Add more GAMES users can play with their friends.

Work on the TEST option during the ONBOARDING process for users.

Create mid-fidelity and high-fidelity wireframes.

Takeaways

Design is an ITERATIVE PROCESS

There are times when you feel like there is nothing stopping you and other times where you feel completely lost in the design process.

Design is a COLLABORATIVE PROCESS

Everyone navigates the world differently, reach out to a friend or family member, they may have a fresh approach to a task.

When in doubt, TAKE A BREAK

When you’re feeling stuck you may want to keep pushing until you figure it out, but instead, take a break, get some air, and come back to your design with fresh eyes.
